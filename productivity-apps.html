Top 7 Productivity Apps You Should Install Today


Boost your efficiency with these essential apps that help students and young professionals stay organized, focused, and productive throughout their busy days.

By Alex Productivity May 6, 2025 8 Comments
Productivity

Between classes, assignments, part-time jobs, and social life, staying productive as a student can feel like an impossible challenge. The right apps can make all the difference, helping you organize your time, minimize distractions, and get more done with less stress.

After testing dozens of productivity apps, I've narrowed down the seven most valuable tools that actually deliver results for students and young professionals. These apps cover everything from note-taking and task management to focus and habit building—all while being budget-friendly (most are free or offer student discounts).

1. Notion (All-in-One Workspace)

Best for: Organizing everything in one place
Platforms: iOS, Android, Windows, Mac, Web
Price: Free for personal use; $4/month for Plus plan (student discount available)

Notion is the Swiss Army knife of productivity apps—a single workspace where you can take notes, manage tasks, create databases, build wikis, and even design simple websites. Its flexibility makes it perfect for students who need different solutions for different classes.

Student Benefits:

  • Create custom dashboards for each class with syllabi, notes, and deadlines
  • Collaborate with classmates on group projects
  • Free Pro plan for students with a .edu email address
  • Thousands of free student templates available online

Features 9.5/10

Value 10/10

2. Forest (Focus Timer)

Best for: Beating phone addiction and staying focused
Platforms: iOS, Android, Chrome extension
Price: $3.99 one-time (mobile), free Chrome extension

Forest turns focus time into a game. Set a timer to plant a virtual tree, which grows while you work. If you leave the app to check social media, your tree dies. Over time, you grow a forest that represents your focused hours.

Student Benefits:

  • Discourages phone use during study sessions
  • Earn coins to plant real trees through partner organizations
  • View your focus history to identify productive patterns
  • Works offline—great for library study sessions

3. Todoist (Task Management)

Best for: Simple but powerful task management
Platforms: iOS, Android, Windows, Mac, Web
Price: Free basic plan; $4/month for Pro (50% student discount)

Todoist keeps your assignments and responsibilities organized with a clean, intuitive interface. Natural language input ("Read chapter 3 next Thursday at 7pm") makes adding tasks lightning fast.

Student Benefits:

  • Color-code tasks by class or priority
  • Set recurring tasks for weekly readings or assignments
  • Share projects for group work delegation
  • Integrates with Google Calendar and other student tools

4. Google Drive + Docs (Cloud Collaboration)

Best for: Cloud storage and real-time collaboration
Platforms: iOS, Android, Web
Price: Free (15GB); $1.99/month for 100GB (student discount often available)

While not new, Google Drive remains essential for students. Its real-time collaboration features make group projects infinitely easier, and automatic saving means you'll never lose work again.

Student Benefits:

  • Work simultaneously with classmates on the same document
  • Access files from any device—no need to carry a laptop everywhere
  • Free for students with .edu addresses at many institutions
  • Built-in OCR lets you search text in scanned PDFs of textbooks

5. Pocket (Read-It-Later)

Best for: Saving articles and videos to view later
Platforms: iOS, Android, Web, Browser extensions
Price: Free basic plan; $4.99/month for Premium

Pocket lets you save articles, videos, and webpages with one click to view later—even offline. It's perfect for when you stumble across relevant research but don't have time to read it immediately.

Student Benefits:

  • Save paywalled articles to read on campus where your institution has access
  • Tag and organize saved items by class or topic
  • Text-to-speech feature lets you "read" while commuting
  • Recommended content based on your saved items

6. Focus@Will (Productivity Music)

Best for: Music scientifically optimized for concentration
Platforms: iOS, Android, Web
Price: Free trial; $7.99/month (student discount available)

Unlike regular music services, Focus@Will provides neuroscience-backed audio tracks designed to extend your attention span. Choose from various instrumental styles based on your personality type.

Student Benefits:

  • Increases focus duration by up to 200% according to user data
  • Tracks adapt to your attention span throughout a session
  • No distracting lyrics or sudden volume changes
  • Pomodoro timer built in

7. Habitica (Gamified Habit Tracker)

Best for: Building good habits and routines
Platforms: iOS, Android, Web
Price: Free with optional in-app purchases

Habitica turns your real-life tasks into a role-playing game. Complete tasks to level up your avatar, earn gold, and unlock features. Miss tasks and your character loses health.

Student Benefits:

  • Makes mundane tasks like laundry or studying more engaging
  • Join "parties" with friends for mutual accountability
  • Custom rewards system (e.g., 1 hour of gaming after 3 study sessions)
  • Dailies feature helps build consistent routines

Why These Apps Work for Students

  • Most offer free tiers or student discounts
  • Designed for mobile-first lifestyles
  • Minimal learning curves
  • Address specific student pain points
  • Work across multiple devices
  • Many support offline use

Potential Drawbacks

  • Premium features often require subscriptions
  • Can be overwhelming to implement all at once
  • Some have steep learning curves (like Notion)
  • Require consistent use to see benefits

Implementation Tips for Students

Rather than installing all seven apps immediately, I recommend this phased approach:

Phase 1 (Week 1-2): Foundation

  • Set up Google Drive for all file storage
  • Install Pocket to stop research rabbit holes
  • Use Forest during study sessions

Phase 2 (Week 3-4): Organization

  • Migrate tasks to Todoist
  • Create a basic Notion dashboard for your classes

Phase 3 (Ongoing): Optimization

  • Experiment with Focus@Will for difficult subjects
  • Use Habitica to build consistent routines

Final Thoughts

The right productivity tools can help you reclaim hours each week—time better spent studying, relaxing, or sleeping. These seven apps represent the best balance of usefulness and affordability for students.

Remember that no app is magic. They're only effective when paired with good habits. Start with one or two that address your biggest challenges, master them, then consider adding others. Small, consistent improvements lead to big results over a semester.
