10 Proven Ways to Extend Your Smartphone's Battery Life


Struggling with battery anxiety? These simple tweaks can help your phone last through even the longest study sessions and campus days.

By Alex TechTips May 6, 2025 8 Comments
Tech Hacks

Image credit: Future

As students, our smartphones are essential tools for learning, socializing, and staying organized. But nothing kills productivity faster than seeing that dreaded 20% battery warning right before an important lecture or study session. The good news? With a few simple adjustments, you can significantly extend your phone's battery life without spending a dime.

After testing these methods across various Android and iOS devices, I've compiled the most effective battery-saving techniques that actually work for student life. Here are 10 proven ways to keep your phone alive longer:

1. Optimize Your Screen Settings

Your display is the biggest battery drain. These adjustments can make a huge difference:

  • Reduce brightness: Set to 50% or lower (use auto-brightness if available)
  • Shorten screen timeout: 30 seconds is ideal for most users
  • Use dark mode: Saves up to 30% battery on OLED screens
  • Lower refresh rate: If your phone has a 120Hz display, consider switching to 60Hz when battery is low

Adjusting these display settings can add hours to your battery life.

Image credit: Digital Citizen

2. Manage Your Connectivity Options

Wireless radios constantly searching for signals drain battery fast:

  • Turn off Bluetooth when not using wireless earbuds
  • Disable Wi-Fi when you're away from known networks
  • Use Airplane Mode in areas with poor signal (your phone works harder to maintain connection)
  • Limit GPS usage to only when needed (set apps to "While Using" location access)

3. Tame Battery-Hungry Apps

Some apps are notorious for draining battery in the background:

  • Facebook, Snapchat, and Instagram are the worst offenders - use their mobile websites instead
  • Check battery usage stats (Settings > Battery) to identify problematic apps
  • Restrict background activity for non-essential apps
  • Disable auto-play videos in social media apps

4. Enable Battery Saver Mode (The Right Way)

Most phones have built-in battery savers, but they work best when used strategically:

  • Android: Enable when battery reaches 30% (or set to auto-enable at 15%)
  • iPhone: Low Power Mode is surprisingly effective - turn on at 40% for maximum benefit
  • Customize restrictions: Keep essential functions like messaging and email active

5. Smart Charging Habits

How you charge affects long-term battery health:

  • Avoid full 0-100% cycles - keep between 20-80% when possible
  • Don't leave charging overnight - use a smart plug or timer to stop at 80%
  • Remove phone case when fast charging to prevent overheating
  • Use original chargers - cheap knockoffs can damage battery cells

Smart charging habits can double your battery's lifespan.

6. Update Your Software Regularly

Those update notifications aren't just annoying - they often include battery optimizations:

  • System updates: Include under-the-hood battery improvements
  • App updates: Developers constantly optimize power usage
  • Security patches: Prevent background processes from draining battery

7. Reduce Notifications

Every notification wakes your phone and uses power:

  • Disable notifications for non-essential apps
  • Batch notifications (Android) or use Scheduled Summary (iOS)
  • Turn off vibration - it uses more power than sound alerts

8. Manage Your Email Sync

Constant email checking is a silent battery killer:

  • Increase sync intervals to 30+ minutes instead of "push"
  • Disable sync for old/unused accounts
  • Use IMAP instead of Exchange if possible (Exchange constantly syncs)

9. Temperature Matters

Batteries hate extreme temperatures:

  • Avoid leaving your phone in hot cars - heat degrades battery capacity
  • Don't charge while gaming - the combined heat is damaging
  • Keep phones warm in winter - cold temperatures temporarily reduce capacity

10. Emergency Battery Saving Tips

When you're desperate for every last percent:

  • Enable grayscale mode (reduces GPU load)
  • Turn off always-on display (if your phone has it)
  • Disable haptic feedback (vibrations use significant power)
  • Carry a portable charger (our student-friendly picks)

Most Effective Tips

  • Screen brightness reduction (up to 2 hours extra)
  • Background app restrictions (1.5 hours extra)
  • Dark mode on OLED screens (1 hour extra)
  • Smart charging habits (long-term benefits)

Least Effective Tips

  • Closing apps (minimal impact on modern OS)
  • Disabling Wi-Fi (unless in poor signal area)
  • Task killers (actually use more battery)

The Bottom Line

With these optimizations, most students can expect 30-50% longer battery life from their smartphones. The best part? None of these tips require technical expertise or compromise core functionality. Focus first on display settings and background apps for the biggest gains.

Remember that battery health degrades over time (about 20% capacity loss after 2 years). If your phone still struggles after trying these tips, it might be time to consider a battery replacement or upgrade.
