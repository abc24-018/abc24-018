Acer Aspire 5 (2025) Review: The Perfect Student Laptop?

4.0/5.0


The Acer Aspire 5 (2025 Edition) delivers impressive performance and premium features at a student-friendly price point, making it one of the best value laptops for college this year.

By Jamie Tech May 7, 2025 24 Comments
Laptops

The Acer Aspire series has long been a favorite among budget-conscious students, and the 2025 edition raises the bar with significant upgrades. Priced at $699 (or often on sale for $649), this laptop promises to handle everything from lecture notes to coding assignments and even some gaming. After three weeks of testing - from all-night study sessions to coffee shop work marathons - here's our in-depth review.

Design & Build: Premium on a Budget

The 2025 model sports a sleek aluminum lid that gives it a more premium look than its price suggests. At just 3.97 lbs and 0.7 inches thick, it's light enough to carry across campus all day without straining your back. The hinge feels sturdy, and the overall construction inspires confidence - important for students who are rough on their gear.

Acer has improved the keyboard significantly from previous models. The backlit keys have 1.4mm travel - deeper than many premium ultrabooks - making long typing sessions comfortable. The touchpad is responsive and supports Windows Precision gestures, though you'll likely want a mouse for intensive work.

Display & Audio: Solid for Student Needs

The 15.6" Full HD IPS display (1920x1080) is bright enough at 300 nits for indoor use, though outdoor visibility could be better. Colors are accurate enough for everyday tasks and casual content creation, though creative professionals might want something with better color gamut coverage.

The dual stereo speakers deliver surprisingly good audio quality for a budget laptop. While bass is predictably weak, mids and highs are clear enough for video lectures and casual music listening. For better sound, plug in headphones via the 3.5mm jack or use Bluetooth 5.2.

Performance: Handles Everything Students Need

The 2025 model comes with Intel's latest Core i5-1250P processor (12 cores, 16 threads) and 16GB DDR4 RAM - a significant upgrade from previous generations. In real-world testing, it handled:

  • 30+ Chrome tabs + Spotify + Word documents with no slowdown
  • 1080p video editing in Premiere Rush (basic cuts and transitions)
  • Statistical analysis in SPSS and R Studio
  • Light gaming (Overwatch 2 at medium settings, 60fps)

The 512GB NVMe SSD boots Windows 11 in under 10 seconds and loads applications nearly instantly. Students will appreciate having both a USB-C (Thunderbolt 4) and two USB-A ports, along with HDMI 2.1 and a full-size SD card reader.

Battery Life: All-Day Power

Acer claims up to 10 hours, and our testing largely confirms this. With moderate use (web browsing, document editing, some video streaming), we consistently got 8-9 hours. Turning down brightness and using battery saver mode can stretch this to 10+ hours for marathon study sessions.

Student Life Tested

We put the Aspire 5 through typical student scenarios:

Lecture Hall Performance

The keyboard is quiet enough for note-taking during lectures. The matte display reduces glare from overhead lights, and the 15.6" screen comfortably fits a lecture slide and notes side-by-side.

Library All-Nighters

The comfortable keyboard and decent trackpad made writing papers less painful. The battery lasted through 7 hours of research and writing with 30% remaining.

Group Projects

The webcam (now 1080p) and microphones performed well for Zoom meetings. Screen sharing worked smoothly, and the laptop handled video calls while referencing documents and browsing.

Dorm Room Entertainment

The IPS panel and decent speakers made movie nights enjoyable. The laptop even handled light gaming during downtime.

The Verdict: 8.5/10 - Best Value Student Laptop

The Acer Aspire 5 (2025 Edition) hits a sweet spot that makes it ideal for students. It offers nearly everything you need for coursework and entertainment at a price that won't devastate your student budget. While it makes some compromises to hit this price point, none significantly impact the core student experience.

For most students, this laptop provides better value than more expensive options. It's powerful enough to last through four years of college, portable enough for daily campus life, and affordable enough that replacing it wouldn't be catastrophic. If you're looking for the best balance of performance, features, and price, the Aspire 5 should be at the top of your list.

Where to Buy

The Acer Aspire 5 (2025 Edition) is available from:

  • Amazon: $699 (frequent sales to $649)
  • Best Buy: $699 (student discount available)
  • Acer Official Store: $699 (with student discount to $659)

Look for back-to-school sales in July-August when prices often drop below $600.
