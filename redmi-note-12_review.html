Redmi Note 12 Pro Review: Mid-Range Marvel for Student Budgets

4.5/5.0


The Redmi Note 12 Pro delivers exceptional value with its stunning AMOLED display, capable camera system, and all-day battery life—making it the perfect smartphone companion for budget-conscious students.

By Alex Tech May 1, 2025 18 Comments
Smartphones

As students, we're constantly looking for devices that offer the best bang for our buck—smartphones that deliver premium features without the premium price tag. Enter the Redmi Note 12 Pro, Xiaomi's latest mid-range contender that promises flagship-like performance at just under $300. But does it actually deliver on these promises?

After using the Redmi Note 12 Pro as my daily driver for three weeks—through lectures, study sessions, social events, and everything in between—I can confidently say this device punches well above its price point. Let's dive into what makes this smartphone a compelling option for students and budget-conscious tech enthusiasts.

Design & Build Quality

The Redmi Note 12 Pro features a sleek, modern design that could easily be mistaken for a much more expensive device. Available in three colors (Midnight Black, Sky Blue, and Forest Green), the phone sports a glass back with a matte finish that resists fingerprints surprisingly well—a practical feature for those of us constantly handling our phones between classes.

At 7.9mm thick and weighing 187g, the phone strikes a good balance between feeling substantial without being too heavy for one-handed use. The frame is made of plastic but has a convincing metallic finish that adds to the premium feel. One notable design feature is the flat edges, which not only look modern but also make the phone easier to grip—particularly useful when taking quick notes during lectures.

Xiaomi has included IP53 dust and splash resistance, which provides some peace of mind when using the phone in light rain or when accidentally spilling your coffee during a late-night study session. While not fully waterproof, this level of protection is rarely found at this price point.

Display

The display is where the Redmi Note 12 Pro truly shines. Its 6.67-inch AMOLED panel with a 120Hz refresh rate makes everything from scrolling through notes to watching educational videos a smooth, vibrant experience. With a resolution of 2400 x 1080 pixels (395 PPI), text appears crisp and legible even during extended reading sessions.

What impressed me most was the display brightness. With a peak brightness of 1200 nits, I had no trouble using the phone outdoors on sunny days between classes. The display also features HDR10+ support, making streaming breaks during study sessions more immersive.

Display Protection

The screen is protected by Corning Gorilla Glass 5, which survived a few accidental drops during my testing period. For extra protection, I'd still recommend a screen protector—especially considering how frequently students handle their phones throughout the day.

Performance

Powered by the MediaTek Dimensity 1080 chipset and paired with either 6GB or 8GB of RAM, the Redmi Note 12 Pro handles everyday tasks with ease. App switching is snappy, and the phone rarely showed any lag during my testing period, even when jumping between resource-intensive apps like Google Docs, Zoom, and multiple Chrome tabs for research.

For students who enjoy mobile gaming between study sessions, the Redmi Note 12 Pro performs admirably with most popular titles. Games like PUBG Mobile and Call of Duty Mobile run smoothly at medium to high settings. While it's not designed to be a gaming powerhouse, it's more than capable of handling casual gaming breaks.

Multitasking & Productivity

The phone's performance particularly impressed me during multitasking scenarios. Split-screen functionality works well for taking notes while watching lecture videos, and MIUI's floating window feature makes it easy to keep a calculator or reference material handy while working on assignments.

One productivity feature I found particularly useful was the IR blaster, which allows the phone to function as a universal remote. This came in handy when controlling projectors during presentations and adjusting classroom AV equipment without hunting for the remote.

Camera System

The Redmi Note 12 Pro features a versatile triple camera setup that proved more capable than I initially expected:

  • 50MP main camera (Sony IMX766 sensor) with OIS
  • 8MP ultra-wide angle lens
  • 2MP macro lens
  • 16MP front-facing camera

The main 50MP camera with optical image stabilization (OIS) is the star of the show, capturing detailed, well-exposed photos in good lighting conditions. I was particularly impressed by its low-light performance—scanning textbook pages and whiteboard notes in dimly lit lecture halls was no problem.

The ultra-wide lens is handy for group photos and capturing wide lecture hall scenes, though there is some noticeable distortion at the edges. The 2MP macro lens feels more like a specification checkbox item than a truly useful camera, with mediocre results even in good lighting.

Video Capabilities

For video recording, the Redmi Note 12 Pro can shoot up to 4K at 30fps or 1080p at 60fps. The inclusion of OIS makes a noticeable difference in video stability, which is particularly useful for recording lectures or creating content for class projects. The audio recording quality is also surprisingly good, with clear voice capture even in noisy environments.

Battery Life & Charging

Battery anxiety is the last thing students need, and thankfully, the Redmi Note 12 Pro delivers excellent endurance with its 5,000mAh battery. During my typical school day—which includes checking emails, taking notes, browsing the web, social media use, and occasional video streaming—the phone consistently lasted from morning lectures until evening study sessions with 20-30% to spare.

On particularly heavy-use days with lots of camera use and GPS navigation around campus, I still managed to get through the entire day without needing to recharge. This is impressive and means one less charger to carry in your already-heavy backpack.

When it does come time to recharge, the included 67W fast charger brings the battery from empty to about 50% in just 15 minutes—perfect for a quick top-up between classes. A full charge takes approximately 45 minutes, which is remarkably fast for a phone in this price range.

Software Experience

The Redmi Note 12 Pro runs MIUI 14 based on Android 13, with Xiaomi promising two years of OS updates and three years of security patches. MIUI has come a long way in recent years, and while it's still feature-packed, it's much less cluttered than previous versions.

There are several student-friendly features I appreciated during my testing:

  • Second Space: Creates a separate user profile for keeping work and personal content separate
  • Game Turbo: Optimizes performance and blocks notifications during gaming sessions
  • Reading Mode: Adjusts screen color temperature for more comfortable reading
  • Digital Wellbeing: Helps manage screen time during intense study periods

The pre-installed bloatware is still a minor annoyance, with several third-party apps coming pre-loaded. Fortunately, most can be uninstalled, and after a quick cleanup, the software experience feels much more streamlined.

Connectivity & Audio

The Redmi Note 12 Pro offers all the connectivity options students might need: 5G support, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.2, and NFC for contactless payments at the campus coffee shop. The call quality is excellent, with clear voice reproduction even in noisy environments like crowded dining halls.

One pleasant surprise is the inclusion of a 3.5mm headphone jack—increasingly rare in modern smartphones. For students who prefer wired headphones or need to connect to audio equipment for presentations, this is a welcome feature.

The stereo speakers produce clear, loud sound that's good enough for watching lecture videos or enjoying Netflix during study breaks. While they lack some bass response, the overall audio quality is above average for this price range.

Student Life Test: A Week in the Life

To give you a real-world perspective on how the Redmi Note 12 Pro performs in a student's daily life, I documented my experience using it through a typical week of classes and activities:

Monday: Lecture Marathon

Starting with a full charge, I used the phone throughout four back-to-back lectures for note-taking, recording audio snippets, and occasional web searches. The battery dropped to only 74% by late afternoon, and the 120Hz display made scrolling through lengthy PDF lecture notes much easier on the eyes. The camera's document scanning mode came in handy for capturing whiteboard diagrams quickly.

Tuesday: Group Project Day

The phone's performance really shined during simultaneous app usage—keeping reference materials open while messaging team members and editing shared documents. The split-screen feature proved invaluable during our Zoom meeting, allowing me to reference notes while seeing my group members. The battery handled 3 hours of hotspot usage for my laptop with minimal drain.

Wednesday: Library Research Session

Spent most of the day in the library with limited charging options. The phone's battery easily lasted through 6+ hours of research, e-book reading, and note compilation. The Reading Mode was particularly helpful during extended study periods, reducing eye strain significantly. Even with heavy use, I ended the day with 35% battery remaining.

Thursday: Campus Photography Project

Used the camera extensively for a visual arts assignment, capturing over 200 photos and several video clips across campus. The main camera impressed with its detail and natural colors, while OIS helped keep videos steady while walking. The ultra-wide lens was useful for architecture shots, though image quality dropped noticeably compared to the main sensor. Despite heavy camera use, the battery still had 25% left by evening.

Friday: Social Night Out

The true test of any phone's low-light photography capabilities! The Redmi Note 12 Pro's night mode captured surprisingly detailed photos at the dimly lit campus concert. The battery easily handled a full day of classes followed by an evening out, including navigation, social media sharing, and music streaming. The fast charging came in clutch before heading out—20 minutes of charging provided enough power for the entire evening.

The Verdict: 8.7/10

The Redmi Note 12 Pro strikes an impressive balance between performance, features, and affordability that's perfectly aligned with student needs. While it's not without minor flaws, the brilliant display, capable main camera, excellent battery life, and fast charging make this phone an easy recommendation for budget-conscious students.

At $299 for the base model, you're getting remarkable value with very few compromises that matter in daily student life. The Redmi Note 12 Pro proves once again that you don't need to spend flagship money to get a smartphone that excels at the things students care about most.

Where to Buy

The Redmi Note 12 Pro is available from:

  • Xiaomi Official Website: $299 (6GB/128GB), $329 (8GB/256GB)
  • Amazon: $299 (occasionally on sale for $279)
  • Best Buy: $299
