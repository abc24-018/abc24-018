Samsung Galaxy A25 5G Review: Premium Features on a Student Budget

4.0/5.0


The Samsung Galaxy A25 5G delivers flagship-like features at half the price, making it one of the best value smartphones for students in 2025.

By Jamie Tech May 7, 2025 18 Comments
Smartphones

Finding a smartphone that balances performance, camera quality, and battery life under $300 is no easy feat. The Samsung Galaxy A25 5G aims to solve this problem for budget-conscious students, offering premium features like a 120Hz AMOLED display, 50MP camera, and 5G connectivity at just $299.

After using this phone as my daily driver for three weeks - through lectures, study sessions, and weekend adventures - I'm ready to share whether it lives up to the hype as the perfect student smartphone.

Design & First Impressions

The Galaxy A25 5G immediately impresses with its premium design that borrows heavily from Samsung's flagship S-series. The glossy plastic back (available in Awesome Black, Awesome Blue, and Awesome Yellow) features Samsung's signature minimalist camera module design.

At 8.3mm thick and 197g, it's slightly heavier than some competitors but feels substantial in hand. The matte plastic frame provides good grip, and the phone includes both a 3.5mm headphone jack and an under-display fingerprint sensor - two features students will appreciate.

The star of the show is undoubtedly the 6.5-inch Super AMOLED display with 120Hz refresh rate - a rarity at this price point. Scrolling through lecture notes and social media feels buttery smooth, and the 1000-nit peak brightness makes it usable even in direct sunlight.

Performance & Daily Use

Powered by Samsung's Exynos 1280 chipset with 6GB/8GB RAM options, the A25 handles everyday student tasks with ease. During my testing, I regularly had 10+ Chrome tabs open alongside Spotify, WhatsApp, and lecture apps without noticeable slowdown.

Real-World Student Testing

For lecture-heavy days, the A25 performed admirably:

  • Note-taking: Handwritten notes with the S Pen (sold separately) were surprisingly responsive on the 120Hz display
  • Video lectures: The AMOLED screen made diagrams and text crisp, while stereo speakers provided clear audio
  • Group projects: Multitasking between Zoom, Google Docs, and research tabs was smooth
  • Campus navigation: 5G connectivity meant fast loading times for maps and transit apps

Camera Performance

The triple camera system (50MP main + 8MP ultrawide + 2MP macro) delivers solid results for social media and document scanning:

In daylight, the main camera captures detailed shots with accurate colors. The 8MP ultrawide is useful for architecture shots or group photos in tight spaces. Night mode produces usable (if not spectacular) low-light shots - perfect for capturing campus events.

For students, the camera's document scanning feature is particularly useful. It automatically detects and enhances textbook pages or whiteboard notes, saving hours of manual editing.

Battery Life - A Student's Dream

The 5000mAh battery is where the A25 truly shines for student life:

Usage Scenario Battery Drain
1 hour video streaming (WiFi, 50% brightness) 7%
1 hour note-taking with screen on 9%
8 hour school day (mixed use) ~60%
Standby overnight (8 hours) 2-3%

With moderate use, I consistently got 1.5-2 days of battery life. The 25W fast charging takes about 90 minutes for a full charge - not the fastest, but adequate when you need a quick boost between classes.

Student Life Test: Three Weeks of Use

To properly evaluate the A25 for student needs, I used it exclusively for three weeks across various campus scenarios:

Lecture Halls & Libraries

The bright, high-resolution display made reading PDF textbooks comfortable even in poorly lit lecture halls. The stereo speakers were sufficiently loud for video lectures, though headphones are better for crowded spaces.

Group Projects

Multitasking between communication apps (WhatsApp, Discord), cloud storage, and productivity tools was smooth. The large screen made split-screen viewing practical for referencing materials while taking notes.

Campus Photography

From capturing whiteboard notes to documenting campus events, the camera system handled most student photography needs well. The ultrawide was particularly useful for architecture students needing to photograph large structures.

Entertainment & Downtime

The AMOLED display and stereo speakers provided an excellent media consumption experience for Netflix breaks between study sessions. Casual games like Among Us ran smoothly, though graphically intensive games required lower settings.

The Verdict: 8.5/10

The Samsung Galaxy A25 5G is one of the most well-rounded budget smartphones for students in 2025. It nails the essentials - great display, all-day battery life, and reliable performance - while adding premium touches like a 120Hz AMOLED screen that are rare at this price.

While it makes some expected compromises (plastic build, slower charging), none significantly detract from the student experience. For $299, you're getting 90% of the flagship experience at half the price - making this an easy recommendation for budget-conscious students who don't want to compromise on quality.
