Sony WH-1000XM5 Review: Are Premium ANC Headphones Worth It for Students?

5.0/5.0


The Sony WH-1000XM5 headphones set the gold standard for noise cancellation and audio quality, but can their premium price be justified for student budgets? We put them to the test.

By Jamie Tech May 7, 2025 24 Comments
Headphones

When it comes to premium noise-cancelling headphones, Sony's WH-1000X series has dominated the market for years. The latest WH-1000XM5 model carries this legacy forward with improved ANC, better call quality, and a refined design. But with a $399 price tag, these aren't an impulse purchase for most students.

After using them daily for three weeks - in dorms, libraries, and during commutes - I've determined whether these luxury headphones can actually be a smart investment for student life.

Design & Comfort: All-Day Study Sessions

Sony has completely redesigned the XM5s with a more minimalist aesthetic compared to the XM4s. The new headband is slimmer and the ear cups have a more oval shape that better fits around ears. The lightweight build (250g) makes them comfortable for extended wear.

The plush ear cushions use soft-fit leather that creates an excellent seal without excessive pressure. I wore them continuously for 6-hour study sessions with no discomfort - a crucial factor for students pulling all-nighters.

The folding mechanism has been removed (a controversial choice), making them slightly less portable, but they come with a sleek carrying case that protects them in backpacks.

Noise Cancellation: Library-Level Silence Anywhere

The ANC performance is where the XM5s truly shine. Sony's new V1 Integrated Processor with dual noise sensor technology creates what is arguably the best noise cancellation available today.

In testing, they completely eliminated:

  • Dorm room chatter
  • Library keyboard typing
  • Cafeteria background noise
  • Bus and train engine sounds
  • Air conditioning hum

The automatic ANC optimizer adjusts cancellation based on your environment and wearing conditions. For students who need to focus in noisy spaces, this feature is transformative.

Transparency Modes

When you need to be aware of your surroundings, the ambient sound modes are excellent:

  • Ambient Sound: Lets in environmental noise naturally
  • Quick Attention: Covers the ear cup to instantly lower volume and let in sound
  • Speak-to-Chat: Automatically pauses music when you start talking

ANC Performance Ratings

Low Frequency Cancellation 10/10
Mid Frequency Cancellation 9.5/10
High Frequency Cancellation 8.8/10
Transparency Mode Naturalness 9.0/10

Sound Quality: Audiophile-Grade Audio

The 30mm carbon fiber drivers deliver exceptionally balanced sound with deep, controlled bass and sparkling highs. The LDAC codec support allows for high-resolution audio streaming when paired with compatible devices.

Key sound features:

  • DSEE Extreme: Upscales compressed digital files in real-time
  • 360 Reality Audio: Creates immersive spatial sound (with supported apps)
  • EQ Customization: Adjust sound profile via Sony Headphones app

For music lovers and audio engineering students, the sound quality justifies the price alone. Podcasts and lecture recordings also benefit from the clear midrange and precise vocal reproduction.

Battery Life: Week-Long Use Between Charges

Sony promises up to 30 hours with ANC on, and real-world testing confirms this:

  • 30 hours at moderate volume (ANC on)
  • 40 hours with ANC off
  • 3 hours playback from 3-minute quick charge

For most students, this means charging just once a week. The USB-C charging is convenient, though wireless charging would have been nice at this price.

Student Life Features

Call Quality

The beamforming microphones and AI noise reduction make these excellent for online classes and calls. In tests, callers reported my voice came through clearly even in:

  • Busy cafeterias
  • Outdoor spaces with wind
  • Dorm rooms with background noise

Multipoint Connectivity

The ability to connect to two devices simultaneously (like your laptop and phone) is invaluable for students switching between lecture recordings and note-taking.

Touch Controls

The intuitive touch panel allows you to:

  • Play/pause with a tap
  • Adjust volume by swiping
  • Skip tracks with swipes
  • Activate voice assistant with a hold

Student Budget Considerations

At $399, the XM5s represent a significant investment for students. Here's how to justify the cost:

Long-Term Value

These headphones are built to last 4+ years of daily use. When amortized over time, the cost becomes more reasonable:

  • $399 ÷ 4 years = $100/year
  • $100 ÷ 12 months = $8.33/month

Where to Find Deals

Look for:

  • Back-to-school sales (August-September)
  • Black Friday/Cyber Monday deals
  • Open-box deals at Best Buy
  • Student discounts through Sony Education Store

    • Alternative Options

    If $399 is too steep, consider:

    • Sony WH-1000XM4 (previous model, often $299)
    • Bose QuietComfort 45 ($329)
    • Sony WH-CH720N ($149, budget option)

    The Verdict: 9.5/10

    The Sony WH-1000XM5 headphones represent the pinnacle of noise-cancelling technology and audio quality. For students who prioritize focus and audio excellence, they're worth every penny - especially if you can find them on sale.

    While the price is steep, the combination of best-in-class ANC, all-day comfort, and exceptional sound quality makes them a transformative tool for study sessions, commutes, and relaxation. If your budget allows, these headphones will serve you well throughout your college years and beyond.
